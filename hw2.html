This plot shows the external population change in Sonoma County from 2018-2019, broken out by level of educational attainment. Inflow refers to people who have moved to the county in the last year, outflow refers to people who have left the county in the past year, and net refers to the net change in the number of people in a given educational attainment class. Based on how the data source, we have one additional category of attainment that was not considered in our first analysis; instead of Bachelors degree or higher, we have the dual categories of Bachelors degree and Graduate or professional degree.

The shape of this chart is almost bell-like; we have little mobility in the high school and less than high school categories, we have the most movement among people with some college, associates, or bachelors, and we have comparatively less among people with advanced degrees. This tracks with our intuition; higher levels of educational attainment are associated with higher economic mobility, which translates to physical mobility.

Less than high school graduate High school graduate/high school equivalency, and Bachelors degree also saw net negative external flux, while Some college or associates degree and Graduate or professional degree saw net positive external flux. The Bachelors degree category disrupts the trend of increasing net inflow with increasing educational attainment; it would be interesting to expand the analysis to other years and see if this trend holds true over time or is an anomaly for 2018-2019.

To further investigate our hypothesis that higher educational attainment = more physical ability, we normalized the plot to represent the proportion of people in each educational attainment group coming to or leaving Sonoma County. Here the trend is even clearer. There are not that many people with graduate or professional degrees, but they have the highest net flux; About 5% of people with graduate or professional degrees turned over between 2018 and 2019, resulting in a net inflow of 0.61% of this sub-population. Interestingly, people with bachelors degrees had a very similar turnover rate, but it resulted in a net outflow.

The normalized plot shows more clearly the lack of educational mobility for people with lower attainment, especially those without high school degrees or equivalents. This population saw a turnover of almost half that of the population with bachelors and graduate/professional degrees. There are multiple possible explanations for this, but some possibilities include: lack of affordable housing options elsewhere/general housing shortages, lack of employment opportunities elsewhere, or close-knit family or community groups.
